About the maker · Burlington, VT

Hi, I'm Bridget. I make furniture
that's meant to outlive me.

Sixteen years ago I left a desk job, bought a used jointer, and started learning to work with wood. Today I run a one-person shop from a converted carriage house in Burlington — and every piece that leaves it is built to be passed down.

My story
How I got here

I grew up in a house full of furniture my grandfather built. A walnut dresser with hand-cut dovetails. A kitchen table whose top had been re-finished so many times the grain showed through like a topographic map. Pieces that were already older than me, and would still be standing long after.

I didn't think about any of it until much later. After college I took a job in publishing, spent six years at a desk in Boston, and slowly came around to the fact that the work I cared about wasn't on a screen. I wanted to make something my hands could finish.

The first piece I ever sold was a maple cutting board — clumsy edges, finish too thick, a knot I should have planned around. The buyer was a friend's mother, and she still uses it. That's when I knew I'd keep going.

Sixteen years on, the work has changed shape — bigger pieces, harder joinery, longer waiting lists — but the why hasn't moved. Every piece is for the person who'll have it twenty years from now.

What I believe

A few things I hold to

Not rules so much as a working philosophy — how I decide what to build, what to charge, and what to turn down.

01
Build it to outlive you.
Solid wood, real joinery, finishes that can be re-applied a generation from now. No MDF, no biscuits where dovetails belong.
02
The wood gets a vote.
A board's grain, knots, and figure decide where it goes. I plan around what the tree gave, not the other way around.
03
No piece leaves unfinished.
If something isn't right, it doesn't ship. If it does ship and isn't right, I'll come fix it. Lifetime guarantee, as long as I'm the one running the saw.
